New User Onboarding for M365 Copilot Mobile

Reframing the first-run experience from a feature tour into a guided first success — so new users feel Copilot's value before they're asked to trust it.

Role
Senior Product Designer
Product
M365 Copilot Mobile
Scope
Strategy · UX · Prompt Design · E2E Flows
Platform
iOS · Android
The Problem

First impressions were breaking confidence, not building it.

New users were landing in Copilot with low expectations and leaving with even lower ones. Chat adoption and retention were both poor, especially on mobile — a surface where attention is short and trust is hard-earned.

Research and data surfaced a clear pattern: users didn't understand what Copilot could actually do for them. Suggested prompts were generic and often ignored. When the first prompt failed or felt irrelevant, trust dropped fast and users rarely returned. Starter SKU users were hit hardest — they had less context for why the AI would be useful.

"If the first interaction feels useful, users will come back. If it doesn't, they probably won't."

The Goal

Design a first-run experience built around a guided first success.

Instead of a generic intro tour, the goal was to reframe onboarding as a moment that helps users actually accomplish something with Copilot on day one. Success looked like:

  • Copilot's value communicated in real, relatable work contexts
  • Users succeeding with their very first prompt
  • A consistent experience that worked across different SKUs and eligibility states
  • Measurable improvement in Week-1 engagement and prompt submission rates

My Approach

Reframing onboarding as a guided first success.

Instead of a generic intro tour, I reframed onboarding as a moment where users feel the value of Copilot before being asked to trust it.

"If the first interaction feels useful, users will come back."

This core idea led to three key design moves: personalized first prompts, progressive setup that runs silently, and clear exits that respect user control at every step.

Key Design Decisions

Three moves that changed the onboarding equation.

01

Personalized first prompts, not generic suggestions

For Premium users, prompts were graph-based — surfacing content connected to their actual work (emails, meetings, docs). For Starter users, lightweight non-graph prompts framed around "help me with my work" were used instead. The focus shifted from showcasing features to demonstrating immediate relevance.

02

Progressive setup — no wall of steps

Eligibility checks and background setup ran silently. Users were only moved to the chat experience when Copilot was actually ready to respond. The same mental model worked across AAD and MSA paid users, removing confusion during early app moments.

03

Clear exits and user control as a design constraint

Users could dismiss onboarding without penalty, at any time. No forced prompts, no dead-end flows. Trust was treated as something earned through design quality — not something extracted through dark patterns or forced engagement loops.

What I Designed

End-to-end flows, not just screens.
